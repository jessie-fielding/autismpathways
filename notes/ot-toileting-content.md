# OT Toileting Sensory Accommodations
Source: ToiletingSensoryAccomodations(2).pdf — provided by OT

---

## Auditory Sensitivities

**What a child may experience:**
- Fear of flushing sounds
- Distress from hand dryers
- Echoes in bathrooms
- Fear of pipes/gurgling
- Fear of automatic toilets activating unexpectedly
- Sensitivity to fan noises
- Distress when multiple bathroom sounds happen at once

**Helpful accommodations:**
- Noise-cancelling headphones
- White noise machines
- Allow the child to leave before flushing
- Play calming music or running water sounds
- Use quieter bathrooms when possible
- Use a sticky note to cover the sensors on automatic toilets
- Bring paper towels or wipes instead of hand dryers
- Warn child before flushing
- Practice flushing from a distance
- Let child flush using foot or tissue if fearful
- Use social stories or videos about public bathrooms
- Visit bathrooms during quieter times
- Let child control flushing from a distance using a long object/string
- Keep bathroom fan off if the sound is distressing

## Tactile Sensitivities

**What a child may experience:**
- Toilet seat feels cold/hard
- Discomfort wiping
- Feeling urine/stool on skin intensely
- Fear of splash with bowel movements
- Avoiding sitting fully on toilet
- Excessive wiping
- Refusal to wipe
- Fear of messy sensations

**Helpful accommodations:**
- Padded or soft toilet seat
- Warm toilet seat before use
- Wipes instead of toilet paper
- Different toilet paper options
- Placing toilet paper in the bowl before bowel movements to reduce splash
- Loose/comfy clothing
- Wearing gloves while wiping to avoid any fear of mess
- Use of a bidet to avoid toilet paper wiping

## Olfactory (Smell) Sensitivities

**What a child may experience:**
- Undesired smells
- Some children are sensitive to cleaning/bathroom product smells

**Helpful accommodations:**
- Scent-free bathroom products
- Ventilation/fans
- Essential oils or wall plug ins
- Mask during bowel movements
- Unscented cleaners
- Open bathroom door
- Preferred scent nearby
- Toilet spray before bowel movements
- Flush during bowel movement if tolerated
- Keep trash emptied frequently
- Let child choose preferred bathroom scent

## Visual Sensitivities

**What a child may experience:**
- Bright lights
- Flickering lights
- Reflections
- Distress with automatic lights
- Visual clutter in bathrooms
- Fear of the flush

**Helpful accommodations:**
- Dim lighting
- Use natural lighting when possible
- Check to see if the lights are flickering (use slo-mo video on camera to check)
- Neutral/simple bathroom décor — reduce wall clutter/decorations
- Sunglasses/hat for fluorescent lights
- Allow child to close lid before flushing
- Cover mirrors temporarily if reflections are distressing
- Use night lights instead of overhead lights

## Postural Stability & Vestibular Sensitivities

**What a child may experience:**
- Feeling unsafe sitting on the toilet
- Fear of falling in
- Standing instead of sitting
- Fearful body posture
- Needing to hold onto walls
- Difficulty relaxing enough to void

**Helpful accommodations:**
- Foot stool, making sure knees are above hip level
- Toilet insert
- Side handles
- Use of mirror in front of them so they can visually see where they are in space and that they are safe
- Adaptive toilet seat
- Use smaller child-sized toilet
- Blowing activities to help relax pelvic floor
- Allow child to sit backwards on toilet if that feels more stable/safe
